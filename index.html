About Me

I graduated from Middlebury College in 2016 with a dual degree in Computer Science and Geography. I was drawn by the complexity of problem solving that an understanding of computer code allowed one to access. Through study of geography, I began to see the world and global systems as data that can be utilized to find truths and use visualization to tell that story. Almost all of my free time in college went towards outdoor leadership. After graduating, I was hired as the Assistant Director of Outdoor Programs and worked to design, plan, and execute large scale programming. While working for Middlebury I acuired certifications in Rock Climbing, White water boating, and Wilderness Medicine. I went on to work the seasonal outdoor circuit, leading trips in the summer and fall, filling my off seasons with gig work in cartography, data analysis, and general contracting. Now I'm finishing my fourth year in Outdoor Education and I am missing the complexity of data analysis. I am looking to enter the data world as an analyist and visualizationist.

General Contractor

The first year after graduating, my friend and I pursued a dream of building an off grid tiny home out of reclaimed material. We found demolition work to reclaim our materials and then built the structure over the course of two weeks. This project gave us enough experience to get hired as general contractors in California for my 2019 winter season. We remodled a school bus into a mobile tiny house and did a complete remodel of an old farmhouse. The complexity of order of operations, design, and construction itself was exhilarating. As a team of two, we raised the house on three sides by as much as 8 inches, reinforced the foundation, stripped the entire house to the studs and reframed nearly every wall, and designed the plumbing and electirc systems. It was the kind of fast paced learning environment I thrive in.

Cartography and GIS

I worked as a site technician on an archeological dig in Western Alaska: Georeferencing the site, mapping and categorizing all finds, and using find locations to create a digital map of the ancient village. I also desgned the maps, charts, and graphics for the Book "Tuning the Kingdom: Kawuugulu Musical Performance, Politics, and Storytelling in Buganda." Most recently, I worked with Glencore to digitize physical map data and create a 3d model of a large copper mine in Canada.
